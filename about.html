About Me

As the name of this website would suggest, my name is Kale Harris. I was born in 1997 in the state of Idaho in the good old U.S.A. My favorite past time is playing video games (big surprise, I know), but I also enjoy going out to play disc golf or sometimes even regular golf. Also, I like to meet up with a couple groups of friends every week to play some Dungeons and Dragons.
During my time in high school, I had an interest in Engineering and took a few classes that gave college credit to get a head start down that path. This is also how I discovered that I don't like Engineering at all, and took a year in college with an undeclared major to explore different avenues and still fill in core class requirements in the mean time.
That is when I eventually found my way to the Games, Interactive Media, and Mobile Technology (GIMM) major. What I liked most about the major is that it provides students with a portfolio of work right out of college, so that they have something to show potential employers as proof that students can do what they claim. I was also drawn to the versatility of the knowledge students gain. There are many avenues we could take: software development, game development, web development, fields involving user experience, digital artistry, or most anything that involves the front end side of projects.
